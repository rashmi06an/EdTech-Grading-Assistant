Data Preprocessing & Scaling

Slide 4 of 10
  • Matrix Flattening: Reshaping 2D $28 \times 28$ matrix coordinates into a 1D flat vector of $784$ features.
  • Min-Max Range scaling: Dividing raw 8-bit integer pixel values $[0, 255]$ by $255.0$ to standard floats $[0.0, 1.0]$.
  • Optimization Benefit: Keeps distance math bounded, speeds up SVM training convergence, and removes scale saturation.
  • Data Splits: Stratified extraction of 20,000 samples for training and 5,000 for validation testing.

Mathematical SVM RBF Kernel

Slide 5 of 10
  • Support Vector Machine (SVM): Identifies the maximum margin decision boundary to separate groups.
  • Non-Linear Mapping: Hand-drawn strokes overlap significantly in flat pixel space. Linear separators fail.
  • RBF Kernel Function: Computes Radial Basis Function mapping:
    K(x_i, x_j) = exp(-γ ||x_i - x_j||²)
  • Kernel Trick: Projects features into infinite dimensions to find a clean separating hyperplane.

Tuning hyperparameter Sweeps

Slide 6 of 10
  • Tuning Method: Stratified 3-fold cross validation (`GridSearchCV`) sweeping $C$ and $\gamma$.
  • Parameters Evaluated: $C \in [0.1, 1, 10]$ and $\gamma \in [0.001, 0.01, 0.1, \text{'scale'}]$.
  • Tuned Solution: **$C = 10$, $\gamma = \text{'scale'}$** yielding **94.98%** mean cross-validation accuracy.
  • Overfitting Check: Gamma value $\gamma=0.1$ collapsed performance (down to 22%) due to localized support vector influence.

Error Analysis: Digit Confusions

Slide 8 of 10
  • Digit 4 vs. 9 Confusion: Most common misclassification. Slanted vertical strokes and loops mimic each other.
  • Digit 3 vs. 8 Confusion: Mathematical coordinate shapes are identical except for an open left loop.
  • Spatial Limitations: Flattening discards structural geometries (intersections, loops). Model relies on pixel overlap.
  • Recommendation: Use HOG descriptors or CNNs to resolve spatial invariance issues.
